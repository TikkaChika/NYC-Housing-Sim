## Parameters

### Simulation Settings

The model uses two core settings:

* `n_sims` controls how many Monte Carlo runs are performed. Each run simulates 10 years of policy costs.
* `horizon_years` sets the length of the simulation window, which is 10 years.

---

### Budget Baselines

The simulation uses two baseline values to anchor the budget environment:

* `total_city_budget_annual` represents New York Cityâ€™s annual budget.
* `baseline_housing_budget_annual` represents the current annual housing-related budget and is used to define the cap.

---

### Cap Settings

This model applies a spending cap based on the housing budget.

* `cap_type` is set to `"housing_share"`, which means the cap is tied to a multiple of the existing 10-year housing baseline.
* `cap_pct` determines how large the cap is. For example, a value like `5.5` means the simulation allows spending up to 5.5 times the 10-year housing budget.

The simulation measures how often the total cost stays below this cap.

---

### Policy Cost Blocks

The model groups the housing agenda into four policy cost areas. Each area has an expected annual cost and a level of uncertainty. During each simulated year, the model draws a random cost from a normal distribution.

The four areas are:

* `rent_freeze_tenant_supports`: covers rent freezes, vouchers, and tenant support programs.
  Mean annual cost is about $0.9B, with $0.3B variability.

* `affordable_units_200k`: includes construction of 200,000 affordable units and expanded NYCHA capital investment.
  Mean annual cost is about $8.0B, with $2.0B variability.

* `tenant_protection`: covers enforcement work, inspections, and legal protections.
  Mean annual cost is about $0.4B, with $0.15B variability.

* `homeowner_tax`: represents net savings from homeowner tax reforms.
  Mean annual savings is about $0.8B, with $0.4B variability.

---

### Interpretation

Each simulation run adds up 10 years of randomly drawn costs and savings. This produces a range of possible 10-year totals rather than a single estimate. The model reports how often the plan stays under the spending cap and how often it exceeds it, giving a sense of fiscal risk.
